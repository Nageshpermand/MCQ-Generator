Detailed History of Telangana
Telangana is a state in southern India that was formed on June 2, 2014, after it was separated from the state of Andhra Pradesh. However, the region has a long and complex history that predates the formation of modern-day India. Here is a detailed history of Telangana:

Ancient History of Telangana State
The region of Telangana has a rich and ancient history, with evidence of human habitation dating back to the Neolithic era. The area was ruled by various dynasties, including the Satavahanas, the Chalukyas, the Rashtrakutas, and the Kakatiyas. The Kakatiya dynasty, which ruled from the 12th to the 14th century CE, is particularly noteworthy for its contributions to Telangana's cultural heritage. The Kakatiyas built impressive monuments and temples, including the famous Warangal Fort.

Medieval History of Telangana State
During the medieval period, Telangana was ruled by the Bahmani Sultanate and the Qutb Shahi dynasty. The Bahmani Sultanate was founded in 1347 CE and was based in the city of Gulbarga. The sultanate was known for its cultural and religious tolerance, and its rulers encouraged the development of Telugu and Kannada literature. In the 16th century, the Qutb Shahi dynasty established the city of Hyderabad as its capital. The Qutb Shahi rulers were known for their patronage of the arts and literature, and they also built several impressive monuments, including the Charminar and the Golconda Fort.
